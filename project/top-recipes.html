Top 3 Filipino Recipes

1. Adobo

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish and cooking process in Philippine cuisine. In its base form, meat, seafood, or vegetables are marinated, browned in oil, and then simmered in vinegar, salt and/or soy sauce, and garlic. It is often considered the unofficial national dish in the Philippines.

How to Cook Chicken Adobo

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp canola oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 pc onion, sliced
  • 1 kilogram chicken cut ups (or your choice of meat)
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 pcs bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns, slightly crushed
  • 2 pc Knorr chicken cubes
  • 1 tsp brown sugar, packed

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté garlic and onions. Then add chicken to the pan and sear on all sides until you have a little browning in the chicken skin.
  2. Pour in vinegar, soy sauce, and water. Add bay leaves, pepper, and Knorr Chicken Cubes. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce heat to a simmer, but do not cover the pan. Continue to simmer for 10 mins.
  3. Remove chicken pieces from sauce and fry in another pan until nicely browned.
  4. Put the fried chicken pieces back into the sauce. Add sugar and let simmer for another 10 minutes or until the sauce has thickened. Serve warm with steamed rice.

2. Lumpiang Shanghai

Lumpiang Shanghai, also known as Filipino spring rolls, is a deep-fried dish consisting of a mixture of ground meat with chopped vegetables like carrots, onions and garlic, wrapped in a thin flour-based crêpe. It can be an appetizer, a snack or a rice topping.

How to Cook Lumpiang Shanghai

Ingredients

  • 50 pieces lumpia wrapper
  • 3 cups cooking oil
  • 1 1/2 lbs ground pork (or your choice of meat)
  • 2 pieces onion, minced
  • 2 pieces carrots, minced
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup parsley, chopped
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 eggs

Instructions

  1. Combine all filling ingredients in a bowl: 1 1/2 lbs ground pork, 2 pieces onion, 2 pieces carrots, 1 1/2 teaspoons garlic powder, 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper, 1/2 cup parsley, 1 1/2 teaspoons salt, 1 tablespoon sesame oil, 2 eggs. Mix well.
  2. Scoop around 1 to 1 1/2 tablespoons of filling and place over a piece of lumpia wrapper. Spread the filling and then fold both sides of the wrapper. Fold the bottom. Brush beaten egg mixture on the top end of the wrapper. Roll-up until completely wrapped. Perform the same step until all mixture are consumed.
  3. Heat oil in a cooking pot. Deep fry lumpia in medium heat until it floats.
  4. Remove from the pot. Let excess oil drip. Serve with sweet chili sauce or Filipino catsup.

3. Sinigang

Sinigang is a Filipino soup or stew characterized by its sour and savory taste. It is most often associated with tamarind as a souring agent, although it can use other sour fruits such as unripe mango, guava or tomato. It is one of the most popular comfort foods in Filipino cuisine.

How to Cook Pork Spare Ribs Sinigang

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork spare ribs, cut into 2‑inch pieces
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 large tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 medium onion, peeled and quartered
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 6 pieces taro root, peeled and halved
  • 1 small radish, peeled and sliced to half‑inch thick
  • 2 finger chilies
  • 10 pieces string beans, ends trimmed and cut into 3‑inch lengths
  • 1 eggplant, ends trimmed and sliced to half‑inch thick half‑rounds
  • 6 pieces okra, ends trimmed
  • 15 pieces large tamarind or 1½ packets or 60 g tamarind soup mix powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 bunch bok choy, ends trimmed and separated into leaves

Instructions

  1. Rinse pork ribs and drain well.
  2. In a pot over medium heat, combine pork and enough water to cover. Bring to a boil, skimming scum that accumulates on top.
  3. Once broth clears, add tomatoes, onion, and fish sauce. Lower heat and simmer for about 1 to 1½ hours or until meat is tender, adding more water as necessary to maintain about 8 cups.
  4. Add taro root and cook for about 4 to 6 minutes or until tender.
  5. Add chili peppers and radish. Continue to simmer for about 2 to 3 minutes.
  6. Add string beans. Continue to cook for about 2 minutes.
  7. Add eggplant and okra and cook for another 1 to 2 minutes.
  8. If using fresh tamarind, boil with 1 cup water in a sauce pan until soft. Mash the tamarind then strain the broth. Add the tamarind broth to the pot. If using tamarind mix powder, add to the pot and stir until completely dissolved.
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Add bok choy and continue to cook for about 1 minute. Serve hot with steamed rice.
