About Our Top Filipino Recipes

Experience the heart of Filipino cooking! These top recipes capture the flavors, heritage, and comfort of the Philippines. From savory Adobo to crisp Lumpia and savory Sinigang, we've got a delicious lineup that will make you feel right at home.

Top 3 Filipino Recipes

Adobo

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish and cooking process in Philippine cuisine. In its base form, meat, seafood, or vegetables are marinated, browned in oil, and then simmered in vinegar, salt and/or soy sauce, and garlic. It is often considered the unofficial national dish in the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp canola oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 pc onion, sliced
  • 1 kilogram chicken cut ups
  • 2 tbsp vinegar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 pcs bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns, slightly crushed
  • 2 pc Knorr chicken cubes
  • 1 tsp brown sugar, packed

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté garlic and onions. Then add chicken and sear until slightly brown.
  2. Pour in vinegar, soy sauce, and water. Add bay leaves, pepper, and Knorr chicken cubes. Simmer for 10 mins uncovered.
  3. Remove chicken and fry until crisp. Put chicken back into sauce, add sugar, and simmer until sauce is slightly thickened. Serve with steamed rice.

Lumpiang Shanghai

Lumpiang Shanghai is a deep-fried Filipino spring roll that can be served as an appetizer, snack, or rice topping. It’s filled with a savory mixture of ground meat, carrots, onions, and garlic, wrapped in a thin flour-based crêpe and deep-fried until crisp and golden brown.

Ingredients

  • 50 pieces lumpia wrapper
  • 3 cups cooking oil
  • 1 1/2 lbs ground pork
  • 2 pieces onions, minced
  • 2 pieces carrots, minced
  • 1 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup parsley, chopped
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 eggs

Instructions

  1. Mix ground pork, onions, carrots, garlic powder, pepper, parsley, salt, sesame oil, and eggs until well combined.
  2. Place 1–1.5 tbsp filling in a lumpia wrapper, fold, and seal with egg wash.
  3. Heat oil and deep fry until crisp and golden. Serve hot with sauce.

Sinigang

Sinigang is a savory and sour Filipino stew. It's famous for its tangy broth, usually made with tamarind, and includes pork, vegetables, and sometimes shrimp. It is a comfort food and a national favorite across the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork spare ribs, cut into 2‑inch pieces
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 large tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 medium onion, peeled and quartered
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 6 pieces taro root, peeled and halved
  • 1 small radish, peeled and sliced
  • 2 finger chilies
  • 10 pieces string beans, ends trimmed
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 6 pieces okra, ends trimmed
  • 15 pieces large tamarind OR 1½ packets tamarind soup mix
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 bunch bok choy, ends trimmed and separated into leaves

Instructions

  1. Boil pork until scum rises, skim it, then add onions, tomatoes, and fish sauce. Simmer until pork is tender (1–1.5 hours).
  2. Add taro and cook until tender (4–6 minutes), then add radish, chilies, and long beans (2–3 mins), followed by eggplant and okra (1–2 mins).
  3. Stir in tamarind or soup mix. Season with salt and pepper. Finish with bok choy. Serve hot!
