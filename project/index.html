Filipino Food

is a melting pot of different tastes from around the world. The Spanish, Chinese, Malaysians, and Americans have all put their culinary stamp on Filipino cuisine, creating a fusion of flavors that is truly one-of-a-kind. It has been making a mark as a world-class cuisine.

Featured Filipino Dish

Adobo

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish and cooking process in Philippine cuisine. In its base form, meat, seafood, or vegetables are marinated, browned in oil, and then simmered in vinegar, salt and/or soy sauce, and garlic. It is often considered the unofficial national dish in the Philippines.
